Transparency and How This Works

Trust matters in mortgage conversations. This page explains exactly how things work, what to expect, and what not to expect.

Why Transparency Matters

The mortgage industry has a reputation for unclear terms, hidden fees, and high-pressure sales tactics. Many people feel uncertain about who they're working with and how their information will be used.

This page addresses those concerns directly. You deserve to know exactly how conversations work, what happens to your information, and what you can realistically expect.

No Referral Marketplace

Your Information Is Not Sold or Shared

This is not a lead generation service or referral marketplace. When you reach out, you're contacting Manny Kashto directly—not a company that will sell your information to multiple lenders.

What This Means

  • Your contact information stays private
  • You won't receive calls from multiple lenders you didn't contact
  • Your personal details are not shared with third parties for marketing purposes
  • You're working directly with one mortgage professional, not being passed around

Bottom line: Your information is used only to provide you with mortgage guidance. It is not sold, shared, or used for any other purpose.

No Pressure

Conversations Are Informational

The purpose of initial conversations is to provide information and answer questions. There are no sales quotas, no urgency tactics, and no pressure to make immediate decisions.

You Decide How and When to Proceed

Every step of the process is your choice:

  • You decide if you want to have an initial conversation
  • You decide if you want to continue after that conversation
  • You decide when you're ready to move forward with an application
  • You can ask questions, take time to think, or walk away at any point

No Aggressive Follow-Up

If you reach out and decide not to proceed, that's completely fine. You won't receive aggressive follow-up calls or emails pressuring you to reconsider. A single follow-up to see if you have additional questions is reasonable; repeated pressure is not.

Bottom line: You're in control of the pace and direction of the conversation. No pressure, no urgency tactics, no aggressive sales approach.

No Promises

Rates, Terms, and Approvals Depend on Qualification

It's impossible to promise specific rates, terms, or loan approval without reviewing your complete financial situation, credit history, and the property details.

What Can Be Discussed

  • General rate ranges: Based on current market conditions and typical qualification scenarios
  • Loan program options: What types of loans might be available based on your situation
  • Estimated costs: Approximate closing costs and fees, subject to final underwriting
  • Qualification factors: What lenders typically look for in terms of credit, income, and assets

What Cannot Be Promised

  • Specific interest rates: Rates depend on credit score, loan-to-value ratio, property type, and market conditions
  • Guaranteed approval: All loans are subject to underwriting, credit approval, and property appraisal
  • Exact closing costs: Final costs depend on property location, loan amount, and third-party fees
  • "Lowest rates" claims: Rate comparisons require identical scenarios and cannot be guaranteed

Bottom line: Honest, realistic expectations based on your specific situation—not promises or guarantees that can't be kept.

How Questions Are Handled

Clear Answers

Questions are answered in plain language, without jargon or evasive responses. If something isn't clear, ask for clarification. The goal is understanding, not confusion.

Honest Limitations

Sometimes the honest answer is "I don't know" or "That depends on factors we'd need to review." When that's the case, you'll hear it directly rather than receiving a vague or misleading response.

Examples of Honest Responses

Question: "What rate can I get?"

Honest answer: "Rates depend on your credit score, down payment, loan amount, and property type. Once I know more about your situation, I can provide a realistic range based on current market conditions."

Question: "Will I be approved?"

Honest answer: "I can't guarantee approval without reviewing your credit, income, and the property appraisal. Based on what you've told me, here are the factors that would support approval and any potential concerns."

Question: "What are the total costs?"

Honest answer: "Total costs include lender fees, third-party fees, and prepaid items. I can provide estimates based on your loan amount and location, but final costs depend on the specific property and services required."

Question: "Is now a good time to buy?"

Honest answer: "That depends on your personal situation, goals, and local market conditions. I can help you understand the financial factors, but the decision about timing is ultimately yours based on what makes sense for your life."

Bottom line: Clear, honest answers—even when the answer is "it depends" or "I need more information to give you an accurate response."

Questions About This Approach?
